NREM Stages (Non-Rapid Eye Movement) encompass
all sleep stages except for REM sleep.

1 - Occurs during the beginning of sleep, with slow
eye movement. This state is sometimes referred to as
relaxed wakefulness. Alpha waves begin to disappear.
People aroused from this stage often believe that
they have been fully awake.

2 - No eye movement occurs, and dreaming is very rare.
EEG recordings show "sleep spindles", which are short
bursts of high frequency brain activity.

3 & 4 - Deep sleep; Stage 3 is the transition between stage 2 and stage 4 where delta waves begin to occur.
In stage 4, delta waves dominate. Dreaming is more common in this stage than in other stages of NREM sleep though not as common as in REM sleep. These are also the stages during which parasomnias most commonly occur.
